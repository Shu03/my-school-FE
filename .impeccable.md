## Design Context

### Users

School administrators, teachers, and students accessing a school management system. Administrators are the primary power users — managing staff, students, and operations. Teachers and students have role-restricted views. Users range from tech-comfortable admins to less tech-savvy staff. Context: daily operational tool used on desktops and tablets during school hours.

### Brand Personality

Professional, friendly, modern — an institutional tool that feels approachable rather than corporate. It should feel like a well-organized school, not a Silicon Valley startup.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Visual tone**: Clean & minimal. Generous whitespace, clear hierarchy, restrained use of color.
- **Theme**: Light + dark mode support.
- **Font**: Nunito Sans Variable — warm, rounded, friendly but professional.
- **Color system**: Mist base (shadcn preset) — cool, calm blues with oklch color space.
- **Anti-references**: Not a Silicon Valley SaaS with gradients and glassmorphism. Not a cluttered old-school ERP. No dated dashboard aesthetics with heavy sidebars and cramped tables.
- **References**: Clean government/education portals. Think Apple-level clarity applied to institutional software.

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration** — Every element earns its place. No ornamental UI.
2. **Calm confidence** — The interface should feel steady and trustworthy, never chaotic or overwhelming.
3. **Hierarchy first** — Use typography weight, size, and spacing to guide attention, not color.
4. **Accessible by default** — WCAG AA minimum. Clear focus states, readable contrast, keyboard navigable.
5. **Progressive disclosure** — Show what's needed now, reveal complexity through interaction.
6. **Tonal layering** — Define sections through background shifts, not 1px borders. Boundaries are felt, not drawn.
7. **Editorial spacing** — Use generous, varied spacing for rhythm. Tight grouping for related items, generous separation between sections. Embrace negative space.
8. **Deliberate motion** — Transitions are slow and intentional (200-300ms). The UI feels considered, not snappy-fast.

### Component Rules

- **No divider lines** within cards or sections. Separate content with spacing alone.
- **No 100% opaque borders** for container definition. Use background shifts or very subtle `border-border/50` when structure requires it.
- **Inputs** use subtle, restrained styling. Bottom-accent focus states preferred over ring outlines.
- **Buttons** use soft, confident transitions. Primary actions feel deliberate.
- **Cards** should be light and airy — avoid heavy ring/shadow combinations.
